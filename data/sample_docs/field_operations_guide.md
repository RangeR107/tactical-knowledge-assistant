# Field Operations Guide

## Chapter 1: Pre-Deployment Checklist

### 1.1 Personnel Readiness

Before any field deployment, ensure all personnel have completed:

- **Medical clearance**: All team members must have a current physical examination within the past 6 months
- **Equipment check**: Individual kit inspection against the standard load list
- **Training certification**: Confirmation of all required qualifications
- **Briefing**: Mission briefing and acknowledgement of rules of engagement

### 1.2 Equipment Manifest

Each team member must carry:

| Category | Item | Quantity |
|----------|------|----------|
| Navigation | GPS device | 1 |
| Navigation | Topographic map | 1 |
| Communication | Personal radio | 1 |
| Medical | IFAK (Individual First Aid Kit) | 1 |
| Sustenance | Water (minimum) | 3 litres |
| Sustenance | Ration packs | 24-hour supply |

## Chapter 2: Movement Techniques

### 2.1 Route Selection Principles

When selecting routes for field movement:

1. **Avoid obvious approaches** — use terrain to mask movement
2. **Plan multiple routes** — primary, alternate, emergency
3. **Identify key terrain features** — high ground, water sources, choke points
4. **Consider time and distance** — plan for realistic movement speeds:
   - Open terrain: 3–4 km/h
   - Dense vegetation: 1–2 km/h
   - Urban environment: 2–3 km/h

### 2.2 Navigation Methods

**Primary navigation (GPS)**
- Always carry fresh batteries
- Record waypoints before departure
- Maintain a GPS log of positions

**Secondary navigation (map and compass)**
- Magnetic declination must be applied
- Resection technique for position verification
- Dead reckoning when visibility is reduced

**Tertiary navigation (terrain association)**
- Identify distinctive terrain features
- Use linear features (rivers, roads) as handrails
- Count paces for distance measurement

## Chapter 3: Observation Posts

### 3.1 Establishing an Observation Post (OP)

An effective OP provides:
- Unobstructed fields of observation
- Cover from view and fire
- Adequate approaches and withdrawal routes
- Communication links to higher headquarters

### 3.2 OP Reporting Format

Use the standard SALUTE report:

| Letter | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| S | Size | 4 personnel |
| A | Activity | Conducting patrol |
| L | Location | Grid 123456 |
| U | Unit | Unknown, civilian clothing |
| T | Time | 0930 local |
| E | Equipment | Light weapons, 2 vehicles |

## Chapter 4: Night Operations

### 4.1 Light Discipline

During night operations:
- No white light unless absolutely necessary
- Use red or blue filters on torches
- Shield all equipment displays
- Be aware of light signatures (watch faces, vehicle displays)

### 4.2 Night Vision Equipment (NVE)

**Care and maintenance:**
- Store in protective case when not in use
- Clean objective lens with approved lens cloth
- Check battery levels before all operations
- Never expose to direct bright light sources

**Effective range (approximate):**
- Gen 2 NVG: 150–200 metres
- Gen 3 NVG: 200–300 metres
- Thermal imaging: 500+ metres depending on conditions

## Chapter 5: Emergency Procedures

### 5.1 Lost Communication Procedures

If communication is lost with headquarters:
1. Attempt to restore comms using all available means
2. Move to alternate communication point
3. Execute the lost-comms plan as briefed
4. Return to last known position if no further orders

### 5.2 Medical Emergency Protocol

**Tactical Field Care (TFC) priorities:**
1. Stop life-threatening bleeding (tourniquet application)
2. Airway management
3. Treat for shock
4. Wound packing and bandaging
5. Prepare for MEDEVAC

**MEDEVAC request (9-Line):**
- Line 1: Location (grid)
- Line 2: Radio frequency and call sign
- Line 3: Number of patients by precedence
- Line 4: Special equipment required
- Line 5: Number of patients by type
- Line 6: Security of pick-up site
- Line 7: Method of marking pick-up site
- Line 8: Patient nationality and status
- Line 9: NBC contamination (if applicable)

## Chapter 6: Debrief Procedures

After every field operation, teams must complete:

1. **Immediate debrief** — within 1 hour of return, covering critical intelligence
2. **Full debrief** — within 24 hours, covering all aspects of the operation
3. **Equipment inspection** — immediate check of all equipment for damage or loss
4. **Medical check** — all personnel assessed for injuries or illness
5. **Documentation** — complete all required reports within 48 hours
