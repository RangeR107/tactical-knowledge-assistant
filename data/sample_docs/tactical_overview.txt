TACTICAL OPERATIONS OVERVIEW
=============================

1. INTRODUCTION TO TACTICAL OPERATIONS
--------------------------------------
Tactical operations refer to the coordinated execution of specific, short-term activities designed to achieve defined objectives within a broader strategic plan. Unlike strategic planning, which focuses on long-term goals, tactical operations are concerned with immediate actions and resource deployment.

Key principles of tactical operations:
- Clarity of mission objectives
- Effective resource allocation
- Real-time decision-making
- Coordination among team members
- Adaptability to changing conditions

2. MISSION PLANNING
-------------------
Effective mission planning is the cornerstone of any successful tactical operation. It involves:

2.1 Objective Definition
Before any operation begins, commanders must clearly define:
- Primary objectives (must-achieve goals)
- Secondary objectives (desirable outcomes)
- Constraints and limitations
- Rules of engagement

2.2 Intelligence Gathering
Intelligence is critical to planning. Sources include:
- Reconnaissance reports
- Signals intelligence (SIGINT)
- Human intelligence (HUMINT)
- Open-source intelligence (OSINT)
- Imagery intelligence (IMINT)

2.3 Resource Assessment
Resources must be catalogued before deployment:
- Personnel availability and skill sets
- Equipment readiness status
- Logistical supply chain
- Communication systems
- Medical support capabilities

3. COMMUNICATION PROTOCOLS
---------------------------
Effective communication is the backbone of tactical operations.

3.1 Radio Communication
- Use standardised call signs
- Employ brevity codes to reduce transmission time
- Maintain radio silence when operationally necessary
- Always confirm receipt of critical messages

3.2 Reporting Structure
Reports must flow through the proper chain of command:
- Situation reports (SITREPs) every hour or as conditions change
- Contact reports immediately upon enemy contact
- Logistics reports at predetermined intervals
- Casualty reports following any engagement

4. FORCE PROTECTION
-------------------
Protecting personnel and assets is paramount.

4.1 Physical Security Measures
- Establish perimeter security at all locations
- Implement access control procedures
- Conduct regular security sweeps
- Maintain a Quick Reaction Force (QRF) on standby

4.2 Information Security
- Need-to-know basis for classified information
- Secure communications at all times
- Operational security (OPSEC) awareness training
- Regular security audits

5. LOGISTICS AND SUSTAINMENT
-----------------------------
Logistics ensures sustained operational capability.

5.1 Supply Chain Management
- Pre-positioning of critical supplies
- Resupply scheduling and routes
- Emergency resupply procedures
- Equipment maintenance schedules

5.2 Medical Evacuation (MEDEVAC)
- Pre-planned MEDEVAC routes
- Landing zone (LZ) identification and preparation
- Casualty collection points
- Medical treatment facilities locations

6. AFTER-ACTION REVIEW (AAR)
-----------------------------
Every operation must conclude with a thorough AAR:
- What was the mission objective?
- What actually happened?
- What went well?
- What needs improvement?
- What actions will be taken to improve future performance?

The AAR should involve all personnel who participated in the operation, creating a learning culture that drives continuous improvement.

7. CONCLUSION
-------------
Tactical excellence is achieved through rigorous planning, effective communication, disciplined execution, and honest evaluation. These principles, applied consistently, form the foundation of mission success.
